About Me

My name is Jiacheng Hu, but I prefer to be called Jackson. I received a M.S degree in Business Analytics from Santa Clara University and a Bachelor degree in Business Economics from University of California, Irvine. Through my undergraduate study and internships, I became more and more certain that data is a crucial part of future business decision making, thus made me to pursue a Master's degree in Big Data and Analytics. Jewelry Retail Salesperson Training

The data of this project is provided by a Zale's, a jewelry Retail chain. Back in 2012, the company offered 3 voluntary training modules to its employees (salesperson & store manager) aimed to improve employeesâ€™ sales & return performance. In 2013, the company further improved the training program and introduced 5 new additional modules related to product knowledge and service and selling skills.

As Business analysts working on this project, we will use the data (2011-13) for following:

  • To quantify the impact of the training programs
  • To assess the change in impact owing to the service years and other parameter for the salespeople
  • To understand the effect of offering additional training modules

    Austin Animal Shelter Outcome Classification

    The data comes from Austin Animal Shelter, and I downloaded the csv files from a Kaggle challenge. The dataset features cats and dogs that are taken in by the Animal Shelter, their breed, color, age and etc., and the eventual outcome of these animals. This analysis focuses on finding out what feature/attribute contributes the most to a good outcome(adoption, return to owner) for these sheltered animals.

    Home Mortgage Approval Prediction w/ machine bias

    With US National Mortgage dataset, there are 11 million+ records that include applications for home purchase, for home improvement, and for refinancing. The objective of this data analysis is to build a machine learning classification model that would be trained on historical data and make predictions on loan approval/denial. Furthermore, this analysis also looks to address the underlying idea of machine bias in current decision support systems that use machine learning/AI.

    Chicago speed violation visualizations

    The dataset is taken from Speed_Camera_Violations-Chicago Data Portal and each record represents the number of violations occurred on a specific day taken by a specific speed camera, starting from July 2014 to April 2019. The mayor of Chicago, Rahm Emanuel, has been extremely proactive in the automated speed enforcement in parks and schools to protect the kids. This visualization project focuses on building a dashboard and interesting visualizations to give proper recommendation to the mayor of Chicago for future improvement of the program.

    U.S. gun violence visualizations

    This is a data exploration of US gun data and custom visualizations based on an article by Vox. In this visualization analysis, the goal is to understand any underlying time trend in gun violence for both homicide and suicide.
